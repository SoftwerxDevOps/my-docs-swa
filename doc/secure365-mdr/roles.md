# Roles & Responsibilities
 
## Security Operations Director
 
The Director of Security Operations is responsible for setting the tone and direction of the SOC, defining its mission, and communicating with senior management and other teams to accomplish its tasks.
 
The Director manages the overall relationships with:
 
- SOC Manager
- SOC Analysts and Engineers
- Account Managers
- Customers
 
---
 
## SOC Manager
 
The SOC Manager is responsible for leading the day-to-day operations of the analyst staff, coordinating with SOC analysts to ensure processes and technology are meeting SOC objectives and metrics.
 
**Responsibilities include:**
 
- Ensuring daily operational processes effectively support SOC operations objectives
- Executing continuous process improvement
- Interfacing with other SOC and outside teams
- Ensuring senior management is aware of any issues or incidents
- Ensuring all SOC staff receives development guidance in accordance with SOC practices and standards
- Managing shift scheduling including regular, holiday, illness, and emergency scheduling
- Conducting shift handovers and briefings
- Owning the successful completion of all daily operational processes and procedures
- Documenting and tracking analyst training requirements
 
---
 
## SOC Analyst
 
The SOC Analyst is responsible for the detailed and repeatable execution of all operational tasks as documented in processes and procedures. Specifically, the SOC Analyst monitors the Microsoft Sentinel Incident Blade for security events, alerts, and incidents — closing or escalating as necessary.
 
**Responsibilities include:**
 
- Rapidly identify, categorise, prioritise, and triage incidents using all available log and intelligence sources including:
  - Firewalls
  - Systems and Network Devices
  - Web Proxies
  - Intrusion Detection/Prevention Systems
  - Data Loss Prevention
  - Antivirus Systems
- Monitor incoming event queues for potential security incidents using Microsoft Sentinel
- Perform initial investigation and triage of potential incidents, escalating or closing as applicable
- Use available SOC tools for historical analysis using Microsoft Sentinel Log Analytics
- Maintain SOC shift logs with relevant activity
- Document investigation results, ensuring relevant details are passed to the customer
- Update and reference Softwerx SOC processes and procedures and review daily intelligence reports
- Mentor junior analysts to improve detection and analytical capabilities within the SOC
- Coordinate with SOC Engineers to tune events and alerts
- Serve as shift subject matter experts on incident detection and analysis techniques
 
---
 
## SOC Engineer
 
The SOC Engineer is primarily responsible for the administration, infrastructure, playbooks, Azure Functions, configuration, and analytic (KQL) rules within Microsoft Sentinel.
 
**Responsibilities include:**
 
- Developing, implementing, and executing standard procedures for the administration, backup, disaster recovery, and operation of Microsoft Sentinel
- Change management for detections, workbooks, playbooks, and notebooks versioning
- Tuning Microsoft Sentinel KQL performance and event data quality to maximise efficiency
- Assisting with data source correlation using Microsoft Sentinel
- Ensuring Microsoft Sentinel components perform as expected, meeting established service level objectives for optimal system uptime
- Communicating with the SOC Manager and analysts to optimise KQL analytics performance
