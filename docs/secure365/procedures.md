# Softwerx secure365 Procedures
 
## Summary
 
The Softwerx secure365 managed detection and response service defines an incident as a series of events that may compromise the integrity of a large number of systems or networks, resulting in catastrophic data loss, prolonged organisational downtime, or major financial impact.
 
This process describes the activities conducted throughout each response phase, enabling the SOC to transition from each phase in an operationally sound manner — involving analysis, triage, case management, and escalation that ultimately engages the customer Incident Response Team (IRT) upon confirming critical or urgent incidents.
 
---
 
## Purpose
 
The purpose of this process is to provide SOC analysts with detailed procedures for the handling of all events or incidents.
 
:::danger Incident Definition
An incident is any of the following:
- Any **malicious event** (hack, leak, breach, compromise, etc.) to IP, PII, confidential data, digital assets, or any element of the network
- Any **violation of security or corporate policy** by anyone granted legitimate access including vendors, partners, employees, guests, interns, or contractors
- Any **malicious event resulting in loss or compromise** to any IP, PII, confidential data, or digital assets caused by anyone with access — or who gained access through a malicious act such as a virus, malware, hack, breach, or worm
:::
 
The daily processes and procedures executed by the SOC demonstrate how the organisation addresses and manages security incidents.
